1. Which of these are association rules? Select all that apply.
    1. People who live further from Downtown are more likely to drive.
    2. Milk that smells and tastes sour is more likely to go bad.
    3. People who tend to eat McDonalds a lot tend to eat at Burger King a lot too.
    4. Students who attend class and labs are more likely to score higher marks on the final exam.
    5. A stove that has be turned on high for at least 5 minutes is most likely too hot to touch without burning yourself.

    A,C, and D.

    Association rules are relationships that are found by looking for patterns in data. For example, people who buy cereal are likely to buy milk.

    Option B is not an association because milk that tastes and smells sour has already gone bad (as a general fact). Option E is not an association because, unless you are wearing oven gloves or your stove doesn't work, you are definitely going to burn your fingers.

  5. Why do we use an apriori algorithm as opposed to just calculating the association rule support on given datasets?  
    Calculating the association rule support on large datasets (~terabytes of data) can be very time consuming. The apriori algorithm is a much smarter approach.

  7. Describe the steps for an apriori algorithm. It can be in point form.  
    1. Start with k = 1, support = n.
    2. Ck = list of itemsets of size k as long as the items are previously determined frequent itemsets.
    3. Determine the support of each itemset in Ck.
    4. Fk = frequent itemsets determined by step 3.
    5. Repeat steps 2-4 until Fk becomes an empty set.
    6. The final output of the algorithm is a large frequent itemset list that combines of all the Fk’s found.

  9. Which of the following statements are true if {apple, orange, pear} is a frequent itemset with 50% support in a list of grocery store transactions? → Mouse
    1. {apple, pear} is also a frequent itemset in this set of data
    2. {apple, orange, pear} does not meet the minimum support threshold
    3. 50% of transactions in the list contain apples, oranges, and pears
    4. All of the above
    5. a) and c) only

    A is true. Each subset of a frequent itemset must also be a frequent itemset; {apple, pear} is subset of the frequent itemset {apple, orange, pear}, so {apple, pear} must also be a frequent itemset.

    B is false. A frequent itemset it a set of items that has at least minimum support; since {apple, orange, pear} is a frequent itemset, we know that it has at least minimum support.

    C is true. % support (X, Y, Z) = % of transactions that contain X, Y, and Z.

  13. Find the item set(s) with a support of 33% and a confidence of 100%.

    T5 = tree, paper, book

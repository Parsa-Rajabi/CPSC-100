Image Representation Questions

RGB Questions

  1. What does RGB stand for?
    RGB stands for red, green, and blue.
  2. How many bits does each color (RGB) represent? How many bits represent all 3 of them in total? In bytes?
    Each of R, G, and B is 8 bits so RGB together would be 24 bits. 1 byte is 8 bits so 24 bits is 3 bytes.
  3. What is the basis of the RGB colour specification?
    RGB is based on 256 intensified for each of the red, green and blue. The shades of white and black can be represented in binary and hex.
  4. What colour is represented by 0b000000001111111100000000?

    Green.

    Colours are represented as 24 bits with the first 8 bits representing the amount of red, the next 8 bits representing the amount of green, and the last 8 bits representing the amount of blue. The 0b in front of 000000001111111100000000 indicates that a number is in binary form and does not affect the RGB value. Looking at 000000001111111100000000, we see that the first and last 8 bits are all 0 meaning that there is no red or blue in the colour. The middle 8 bits are all 1's meaning that there is as much green in the colour as possible.

  5. How do various device screens make all the colors they show?
    They mix red, green, and blue light.

Bitmap, Vectors and Pixel Questions

  1. What are two ways to represent images?
    Bitmaps and vectors
  2. How do vector images differ from bitmaps? What is an advantage of vector images over bitmaps?
    While bitmaps define images as grids of pixels, vectors describe them based on points and how they're connected. An advantage of this is that vector images don't pixelate when you zoom in on them.
  3. What is the definition of bitmap image?
    Bitmap is a method of storing images as pixels.
  4. What is a pixel?
    Pixels are little components of an image. Each pixel's colour is represented in RGB.
  5. How do you construct a bitmap image representation?
    To construct a bitmap image, you will need the dimensions of the image as well as the image's pixels.
  6. How do you construct a vector image representation?
    Use a number sequence to make up the dots that construct the object.

True or False Questions

  1. True or false: Lossy compression modifies similar colours to the same colour.
    True
  2. True or false: Vector images describe objects as a sequence of lines and simple shapes.
    False
  3. True or false: RGB cannot show shades of black and white.
    False
  4. True or false: Black is the full intensity of each colour of light.
    False. Black is the absence of light. In RGB representation, black is represented as 0b000000000000000000000000 (0 for R, 0 for G, and 0 for B).
  5. True or false: White is represented by 000000000000000000000000 (24 zeroes) in Binary and 000000 in hex.
    False. White is the presence of all light and is represented by (11111111 11111111 11111111)2 or 0xFFFFFF. Note that the spaces in the binary number are for you to quickly discern the number of digits and are not generally included when presenting binary numbers.

Facts and Conceptual Image Questions

  1. What is the difference between lossless and lossy compression?

    Lossless compression only collapses "runs" of identical intensities so that the image looks unchanged whereas lossy compression modifies areas with similar colours to be the same colour altering the appearance of the image.

    If you use lossless compression, you can obtain your original image at its original quality if you decompress the file. If you use lossy compression, you will lose data so you cannot revert back to the image's original quality.

  2. How do computers store images?
    Computers commonly store images as bitmaps. However, they also sometimes store images as vectors.

  7. Which of the following statements is false?
    1. A colour with an RGB value of #0114FF will be Blue
    2. Vector images describe images based on how different points are connected
    3. A run of identical colours can be compressed into a lossy compression

    E is true. In the RGB value FF01FE, both R and B have high values meaning the resulting colour has a lot of red and blue. Since FF and FE are roughly the similar, we can consider the resulting colour to be a fairly even mix of red and blue which makes purple.

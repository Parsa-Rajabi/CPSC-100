  6. What's the relationship between the Chinese Room Test and the Turing Test?
    1. The Turing Test supports the Chinese Room test
    2. The Chinese Room Test supports the Turing Test
    3. The Turing Test argues against the Chinese Room Test
    4. The Chinese Room Test argues against the Turing Test
    5. The Chinese Room Test and the Turing Test have no relation

    D. The Turing Test is a test of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behaviour equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, a human. The Chinese Room Test argues that a program can not give a computer a mind, consciousness or understanding. Therefore, it can not be "smarter" than a human. As a result, the Chinese Room Test argues against the Turing Test.

  7. What is the most reasonable explanation as to why CleverBot could be a possible contestant for passing the Turing Test?
    1. CleverBot wouldn't pass the Turing Test because it's a robot not a human.
    2. CleverBot replies to questions with answers that have already been programmed in by the creators (it chooses the best reply out of the given answers); therefore making its answers humanistic.
    3. CleverBot uses past human answers to answer questions in new interactions, which make it seem human because it knows everyday language.
    4. It breaks down the question into keywords and phrases, applies predicates on those keywords, searches information about the keywords, and ranks the results.

    A: False. The Turing Test is unrelated to whether or not something is actually a machine or a human. To pass the Turing Test, a machine has to answer questions in a way that would fool a human into thinking that he/she is conversing with another human.

    B: False. This is what Eliza does. It is easy to see that she is a machine because her answers do not vary much and often draw upon portions of your previous answer.

    C: Correct. Cleverbot replies very similarly to a human (knows slang etc.) because it uses conversations from humans.

    D: False. This is how Watson, the Jeopardy machine, thinks (refer to the videos we watched in class).
